About Mycal

Two bodies of water, two bodies of work.

Mycal makes story-driven folk-electronic music shaped by lived places and real communities: the firelit temporary city of Black Rock City, and the slower family geography of the north shore of Clearlake. The songs come out of camps, kitchens, docks, dust, memory, and the long afterglow of places that keep changing you.

Based in Petaluma, Mycal is a long-time Burner, a member of Reared in Steel, and the creator of Cronosonics, a format where essays and songs are designed to make ideas stick.

Ashes and Echoes

A gift for the Playa

Ashes and Echoes grew out of life inside the Burning Man world: camp builds, metalwork, absurd conversations, sunrise recovery, and the emotional architecture that forms when a temporary city starts to feel like home. As a member of Reared in Steel, Mycal wrote from inside that lived experience rather than from the outside looking in.

The album was made as a gift for Burning Man 2025, a record for the people who know what it means to come back coated in dust, carrying stories that only make sense after enough firelight. It moves between humor, devotion, exhaustion, grief, and joy, treating playa culture as both spectacle and intimate human practice.

The record also arrived during the sudden emergence of new creative tools, which helped compress the making process without changing the reason for the work. The point was never the tools themselves. The point was to get the songs to the Playa while the feeling was still hot.

Cronosonics

Essays that sing

Cronosonics is a creative format invented by Mycal: essays paired with companion songs so complex ideas become more memorable, emotional, and usable. The essay carries the full explanation. The song compresses the framework into rhythm, image, and repetition, turning analysis into something you can actually remember later.

Chronos points to time and durability. Sonus points to sound. The dropped h makes the name move more cleanly, like a title that already knows its own chorus.

Origin

The format came out of an accidental experiment: write an essay, distill its core ideas into a rough song, publish both, then notice readers quoting not just the prose but the lyrics when they needed the idea again.

Method

Essay first. Distill the core frameworks. Turn them into verses and chorus. Match the tone of the music to the tone of the thinking. Release both together so one explains and the other reinforces.

Why It Works

People forget bullet points. They do not forget choruses. Cronosonics uses dual encoding: prose engages analytical attention, while music recruits rhythm, feeling, and mnemonic memory.

Cronosonics is not background music for a blog. It is a way of building ideas with a second nervous system: one part argument, one part recall engine.

North Shore Kinda Heaven

The lake, the house, the family record

North Shore Kinda Heaven is an upcoming concept album, planned for release on June 15, 2026, drawn from 22 years of life at a lake house on the north shore of Clearlake. It is a record about the places a family returns to often enough that they begin shaping the family back.

The songs trace summers on the water, favorite coves, routines around the dock, kids growing up at the lake, and the larger orbit of relatives and friends who passed through the big blue house and made it part of their own map too. Where Ashes and Echoes leans toward public ritual, fire, and chosen community, North Shore Kinda Heaven turns toward water, kinship, memory, and home.

The album is deeply personal. Most of the writing is Mycal's, with two tracks shaped primarily by MaryCal: Clearlake Summer with the Kids and Lake House Blue. Those songs will be credited as Mycal, featuring MaryCal, marking her voice as part of the emotional center of the record rather than a footnote beside it.

  • Setting: the north shore of Clearlake and the big blue house on the lake.
  • Theme: family memory, place attachment, and the long arc of raising kids by the water.
  • Contrast: playa community and fire on one side, lake family and home on the other.

The throughline

Across the records, the constant is not genre so much as fidelity to place. One body of work is forged in dust, steel, and fire. The other is held by water, family, and time. Together they sketch the full shape of the project: music made from the communities that built the life around it.
